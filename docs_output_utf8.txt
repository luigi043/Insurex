Insured Asset Protection Register/Exchange
Purpose:
The purpose of the register is to provide finance companies a way to help them mitigate the risk associated with financed asset not being insured through actions of the debtor. The risk is that the asset is destroyed or stolen while there is no insurance covering the asset.
Parties:
The finance company (usually a bank)
The debtor (the client of the bank)
The insurance company providing the cover
Benefits:
To the finance company, their asset can be replaced in the event of total destruction or theft, or money is available to settle the debt.
To the debtor, money is available to settle the outstanding debt or the asset is replaced.
To the insurance company, the register will assist business retention by reducing policy cancellations.
The register/exchange:
The register seeks to provide a mechanism/means of ensuring that financed assets remain fully insured for the duration of the finance period. There are two main classes of assets that need to be monitored, the Motor vehicles and Non-Motor vehicles classes. 
Motor vehicles: This class includes all types of vehicles, cars, trucks, mechanical horses, buses, trailers etc. and can be adapted to include other vehicles like boats, yachts, aircraft and ships. For the purpose of getting the project off the ground, we will focus on road going vehicles.
Non-motor vehicles: This class includes all assets not covered under the Motor vehicle class, such as commercial and domestic buildings, furniture, tools, equipment, stock, etc.
The current situation:
Motor vehicle class is used as an illustration.
A client buys a car after obtaining finance from a finance company such as a bank. The bank advances the loan on the basis that fully comprehensive insurance shall be maintained throughout the period of finance, which ranges from 1 to 72 months. The client is required to provide proof to the bank that the asset is insured. The proof is in the form of a letter from the insuring company to that effect. The insurance company notes on the insurance policy the interest of the bank in the insured asset. Should the policy get cancelled before the loan period is up, the insurance company undertakes to inform the bank accordingly, but there is no obligation to do so.

Debtors are continuously "shopping" for cheaper insurance cover and may also change insurers from time to time. On changing insurers, most debtors do not notify the finance company of the details of the new insurer. With a change in insurer and the non-payment of premiums this exposes the finance company to risk. 
The financial risk:
It is a practice of many clients to cancel the insurance on the asset without taking out a new replacement policy. This exposes the bank to a financial risk because, if the asset is destroyed or stolen whilst uninsured, the client does not want to continue to service the loan.
Asset Protection Register/exchange (APR):
A register of all the financed assets of participating banks for which insurance is a prerequisite. 
At inception of the loan, the identifying details of the asset (a motor car) are recorded on the register. These details include the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) and the registration number of the car, together with the inception date and the period of the loan, the identity of the insuring company and their policy number.
Activities on the policy that pose a risk to insurance:
The following events will trigger an alert to the APR, which in turn will alert the finance company.
Non-payment of the premium
Cancellation of the policy
Removal of the asset from the policy
Change of address
Change of registration number
Reduction in the amount of insurance cover
It is obvious from the above that the co-operation of the insurance company is crucial to the proper functioning of the APR. Also, APR must be as un-intrusive, from the point of view of the insurance company, as possible to encourage participation.
Conclusion:
The above is merely an initial thought about APR, no doubt there will be many improvements before the system is functional. Whatever the final form the system takes, there is a need for it, and its benefits extend beyond the mere protection of the financed assets.

InsureX Platform - System Description
Summary Statement (for GitHub / reviewers)
InsureX is a multi-tenant B2B SaaS platform that integrates with insurers to continuously monitor insurance compliance on bank-financed assets, detect non-compliance events in near real time, and orchestrate engagement workflows to restore compliance. It provides dashboards, audit trails, and reporting to financial institutions and insurers, functioning as a critical control layer between lending and insurance ecosystems.

1. Purpose of the Platform
InsureX is a B2B SaaS platform designed to address a systemic risk gap between financial institutions (banks and asset financiers) and insurance companies where assets financed by banks are contractually required to remain insured, but where:
Banks do not have visibility of ongoing insurance compliance
Insurers do not have structural tools to prevent policy lapse
Customers frequently default on insurance obligations once financing is approved
Insurance lapses are often only discovered after a loss has occurred
The platform's purpose is to provide:
A trusted, automated control layer that continuously monitors insurance compliance on financed assets and triggers timely intervention when non-compliance occurs.
It is not an insurance product.It is a risk management, monitoring, and engagement orchestration platform.

2. Business Problem Being Solved
For Financial Institutions:
Insurance is verified at origination but not continuously monitored
Policy lapses convert insured collateral into uninsured credit exposure
This creates unexpected losses, governance gaps, and regulatory risk
For Insurers:
Customers lapse policies once loan is secured
Retention interventions occur too late
High churn undermines customer lifetime value (LTV)
Acquisition costs are repeatedly wasted
InsureX solves this by acting as a real-time monitoring and engagement hub between:
Insurers
Banks
Customers

3. Core Functional Objective
Ensure that every financed asset remains continuously insured, and that any lapse, reduction in cover, or risk change is detected and acted upon immediately.
This is achieved through:
Data integrations with insurers
Event detection logic
Engagement orchestration (notifications, follow-ups)
Audit logging
Reporting and dashboards for institutional clients

4. High-Level System Architecture (Conceptual)
The platform sits between three parties:
Insurer systems    InsureX platform    Bank dashboards
                       
                  Customer engagement (SMS / email / call)
Data flows:
Insurers provide policy status data (via API, file feed, or batch updates)
InsureX evaluates data against business rules
When non-compliance is detected, InsureX:
Flags an event
Triggers engagement workflows
Notifies the relevant institution
All activity is logged for reporting and audit

5. Key Functional Components
5.1 Data Ingestion Layer
The platform must support ingesting data from insurers in multiple formats:
API integrations (preferred)
Secure file upload (CSV, JSON, XML)
Scheduled batch feeds
Typical data fields:
Policy number
Client identifier
Asset identifier
Cover status
Premium payment status
Policy effective date
Cancellation date (if any)
Sum insured
Risk address

5.2 Rules Engine (Event Detection)
The system evaluates incoming data against predefined business rules such as:
Policy payment overdue beyond X days
Policy cancelled
Cover reduced below minimum required
Risk address changed
Asset removed from cover
When a rule breach is detected, the system generates a:
Non-Compliance Event
Each event must have:
Unique event ID
Timestamp
Event type
Severity level
Associated client / asset
Associated institution(s)

5.3 Workflow & Engagement Engine
Once an event is created, the platform orchestrates engagement actions such as:
Automated SMS notification
Automated email notification
Task creation for call-centre follow-up
Escalation if unresolved after X days
This component must:
Track each engagement attempt
Record timestamps and outcomes
Support multiple attempts
Allow configuration per institution
The system should support both:
Fully automated engagement
Human-assisted engagement via an operator dashboard

5.4 Institution Dashboards
Separate dashboard views should exist for:
Financial institutions (banks / financiers)
Insurers
Internal InsureX operations team
Dashboards should provide:
Portfolio overview
Active non-compliance events
Resolution status
Average time to resolution
Historical trends
Audit logs

5.5 Audit & Compliance Layer
Every action in the system should be logged, including:
Data received
Events generated
Notifications sent
Manual interventions
Status changes
This enables:
Regulatory defensibility
Client audit reviews
Dispute resolution
SLA monitoring

6. Commercial Logic Embedded in the Platform
The platform supports a commercial model where:
Clients pay a fixed platform subscription
Additional fees are triggered per non-compliance event handled
Therefore, the system must be able to:
Count events by institution
Distinguish event types
Track resolution outcomes
Produce billing-ready reports

7. Security & Data Handling Expectations
Given the nature of the data, the platform must:
Enforce strong authentication and role-based access control
Segregate data between institutions (multi-tenant isolation)
Encrypt data in transit and at rest
Maintain audit logs
Support POPIA-aligned data protection practices (South Africa)

8. What the Platform Explicitly Does NOT Do
To avoid misinterpretation:
The platform does not:
Underwrite insurance
Price insurance products
Provide financial advice
Replace broker functions
Force customers into specific insurers
Make credit decisions
It is a monitoring, orchestration, and control platform, not a financial product.

9. Typical Use Case Flow
Bank finances a vehicle for a customer
Customer provides insurer details
Insurer feeds policy status data to InsureX
Customer misses a premium payment
Insurer feed reflects policy in arrears
InsureX detects non-compliance
InsureX sends SMS/email to customer
Customer pays outstanding premium
Insurer feed updates to compliant
InsureX marks event as resolved
Bank dashboard reflects restored compliance

10. Intended Outcomes
If the platform is functioning correctly, it should result in:
For banks:
Fewer uninsured losses
Better collateral integrity
Improved governance and audit outcomes
For insurers:
Lower churn
Higher policy retention
Improved customer lifetime value
For customers:
Reduced accidental lapses
Better awareness of obligations
Fewer catastrophic uninsured losses

3. Mandatory Patterns
Controllers
No business logic
Validate input
Call service
Return View / PartialView / Json / ProblemDetails
Services
All business logic
Enforce tenant + permissions
Orchestrate workflows
Use repositories + HttpClients
Own transactions
Repositories
EF Core only
No business logic
Always tenant-scoped

4. Multi-Tenancy Standard
Every tenant-scoped table includes:
TenantId UNIQUEIDENTIFIER NOT NULL
Tenant resolved from:
Auth claims (preferred)
API key mapping (system clients)
Enforcement layers:
TenantContext middleware
EF Core global query filters
SQL Server RLS using SESSION_CONTEXT('TenantId') (recommended)
Non-negotiable: No endpoint accepts tenant ID from client input.

5. Security Standards
Portal users
Cookie authentication
Roles: Admin, BankUser, InsurerUser
Claims include TenantId, OrgId, Roles
System integrations
OAuth2 Client Credentials (preferred)
OR signed webhooks (HMAC SHA-256)
Required headers for webhooks:
X-Webhook-Signature
X-Webhook-Timestamp
X-Webhook-Nonce
Idempotency-Key
Secrets
Never in code or config
Stored in Azure Key Vault only

